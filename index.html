The Al-Aman Approach

What makes Al-Aman different.

Many providers simply hand you a Wasiat and stop there. We go further. Under Shariah, a Wasiat can only distribute up to one-third of what you leave behind, and only to non-heirs. So if your wishes go beyond that — providing for your own heirs, or giving more than one-third — a Wasiat alone cannot honour them. That's where our guided approach comes in.

01

Wasiat — Your Islamic Will

A Wasiat is your written declaration of how up to one-third of what you leave behind should be distributed after you pass — to non-heirs such as adopted children, non-Muslim relatives, friends, or charitable causes (sadaqah jariyah). It also lets you appoint a guardian for your children. The remaining two-thirds is distributed to your heirs according to Faraid.

Hibah — A Gift Given in Love

A Hibah is a voluntary gift of ownership made during your lifetime, given freely and without expectation of return. Unlike a Wasiat, a Hibah has no one-third limit and no restriction on who may receive it — including your own heirs. Because the gift sits outside what you leave behind, it is not subject to Faraid. This makes Hibah the ideal way to provide for a spouse, ensure a home stays with a chosen child, secure a business succession, or care for a loved one with special needs.

The Complete Guide

Wasiat and Hibah: a complete guide.

وصية

What is a Wasiat?

A Wasiat is an Islamic will that takes effect after death. Under Shariah, it allows you to direct up to one-third of what you leave behind to non-heirs — people or causes who would not otherwise inherit under Faraid, such as adopted children, non-Muslim family members, friends, or charity. A Wasiat is also where you appoint guardians for your children. The remaining two-thirds passes to your rightful heirs according to Faraid.

هبة

What is a Hibah?

A Hibah is a gift of ownership made during your lifetime, given freely and without expecting anything in return. Because it takes effect while you are alive and moves the asset outside what you leave behind, it is not bound by the one-third limit or by Faraid — and it can be given to anyone, including your own heirs. Hibah is often used to provide for a spouse, keep a family home with a chosen child, plan a business succession, or care for a dependant with special needs.

معًا

How they work together

Many families need both. A Wasiat handles your wishes for non-heirs within the one-third limit; a Hibah handles everything beyond that — your heirs, or gifts larger than one-third. Used together, and guided by Shariah principles, they let you honour every wish while staying faithful to Islamic law.
